About Me

I am a PhD student in Physical Oceanography and Climate Science, jointly hosted by the University of Southampton and the National Oceanography Centre (NOC), Southampton, UK. I am part of the INSPIRE Doctoral Training Partnership (Cohort 5) and the Marine Systems Modelling group at NOC.

My PhD research focuses on understanding how ENSO (El Niño–Southern Oscillation) has evolved and will continue to evolve in a warming climate, and what implications this has for ocean-driven climate extremes. I work with high-resolution coupled climate models (CMIP6 HighResMIP) and atmosphere-forced ocean-only simulations, employing large ensemble analysis, model inter-comparison, and idealised sensitivity experiments.

Prior to my PhD, I completed a BS-MS dual degree in Earth and Environmental Sciences (with a minor in Data Sciences and Engineering) from IISER Bhopal, India. My MS thesis explored variability of ocean currents in high-resolution OGCMs and their relationship with climate modes, supervised by Prof. Paul Myers (University of Alberta) and Dr. Pankaj Kumar (IISER Bhopal).

Research

My PhD project sits at the intersection of ocean modelling, climate dynamics, and machine learning.

PhD Project

Dynamical Evolution of ENSO in a Warming Climate

Investigating how the dynamics of ENSO — including event frequency, amplitude, spatial pattern, and teleconnections — are changing under anthropogenic warming, and how these changes propagate to oceanic climate extremes. I work with large multi-model ensembles from CMIP6 HighResMIP and forced ocean-only simulations, focusing on vertical coherence of sub-surface temperature anomalies and their surface manifestation.

ENSO CMIP6 HighResMIP Ocean Temperature Climate Extremes
Past Research

Ocean Currents & Climate Modes (MS Thesis)

Analysed variability of ocean currents in high to very high resolution OGCMs (ocean general circulation models) and investigated historical changes in oceanic and sea-ice conditions around Canada, with a focus on the relationship between current variability and large-scale climate modes.

OGCMs Sea Ice Arctic Ocean
Past Research

AI/ML for Spatial Downscaling of Climate Data

Applied super-resolution convolutional neural network (SRCNN) techniques to spatially downscale gridded IMD (India Meteorological Department) precipitation and climate data, contributing to improved regional climate assessments.

SRCNN Downscaling Precipitation Deep Learning

Research Software & Projects

Open-source tools and datasets from my research.

VIZ3D-ENSO

An interactive 3D visualization dashboard for exploring ENSO composites from coupled and ocean-only model simulations. Enables intuitive inspection of subsurface temperature anomaly structures across the Pacific basin.

Python ENSO Visualization Climate Models

SNCS_IMD_SISR

Spatial downscaling of IMD gridded precipitation data using AI/ML-based super-resolution image reconstruction techniques. Supports the development of higher-resolution regional climate datasets over India.

Python Deep Learning SRCNN Precipitation

GlobalSST_Analyzer

An interactive Python tool for plotting and analysing daily and monthly mean global sea surface temperature (SST) data. Useful for rapid exploration of SST trends, anomalies, and seasonal cycles from global datasets.
