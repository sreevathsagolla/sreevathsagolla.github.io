About Me

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Southampton and the National Oceanography Centre, studying the evolution of the El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO) in a warming climate and its impact on climate extremes. My work combines eddy-resolving high-resolution climate model simulations with targeted sensitivity and attribution experiments to unravel the mechanisms driving ENSO variability.

My broader research interests span physical oceanography, large-scale ocean dynamics, ocean–atmosphere coupling, climate variability and change, ocean modelling, and AI/ML applications in ocean and climate science.
