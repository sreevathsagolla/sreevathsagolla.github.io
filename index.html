About

Research & Education Background

I study the dynamics of the El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and how it is reshaped under anthropogenic warming. My work combines eddy-resolving, high-resolution climate model simulations with targeted sensitivity experiments to disentangle the mechanisms driving tropical Pacific variability and its global teleconnections.

My research sits at the intersection of physical oceanography, ocean–atmosphere coupling, climate variability and change, and ocean modelling, with growing interests in applying AI/ML techniques to climate science problems.

Research Journey

PhD Researcher

University of Southampton & National Oceanography Centre

Sep 2023 – Present

Investigating the evolution of ENSO in a warming climate using eddy-resolving model simulations and targeted sensitivity experiments. Combining high-resolution climate model simulations with attribution experiments to unravel mechanisms driving ENSO variability and its broader impacts. Under INSPIRE Doctoral Training Partnership programme, funded by NERC, UK Research & Innovation.

Advisors: Dr. Joël Hirschi, Dr. Adam Blaker, Dr. Jennifer Mecking & Dr. Stephen Kelly (NOC); Prof. Robert Marsh (University of Southampton)

Visiting Researcher

Climate & Global Dynamics (CGD) Laboratory, NSF NCAR, Boulder, CO, USA

Aug 2025 – Oct 2025

Evaluated resolution dependence and future reorganisation of Indo-Pacific variability in CESM MESACLIP simulations across different global warming scenarios. Assessed projected changes in Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD), Indonesian Throughflow (ITF), and inter-basin IOD-ITF-ENSO interactions. Analysed moderate to very high-resolution (0.1°) ocean-atmosphere coupled CESM simulations.

Supervisors: Dr. Gökhan Danabasoglu (Head, Oceanography Section), Dr. Isla Simpson (Head, Climate Analysis Section), Dr. Fred Castruccio (Scientist IV, Oceanography Section)

Research Intern — Mitacs Globalink Fellowship

Geophysical Fluid Dynamics & Modelling Lab, University of Alberta, Edmonton, Canada

May 2022 – Aug 2022

Analysed the North Atlantic Current (NAC) representation in high-resolution NEMO model configurations at varying spatio-temporal scales. Examined SST and SSH contours from different model setups; calculated section transports with comparisons to satellite/reanalysis data. Work continued into MS thesis.

Supervisor: Prof. Paul G. Myers (GFDL Lab, Dept. of Earth and Atmospheric Sciences)

Research Intern — Deep Learning-Based Downscaling (GANs)

Climate & Glacier Modelling Lab (CGMLI), Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Bhopal, India

Aug 2021 – Mar 2022

Developed a custom Super-Resolution GAN (SRGAN) to spatially downscale IMD gridded rainfall data from 1.00° to 0.25° resolution over India. Led the full technical implementation: architecture design, coding, testing and hyperparameter tuning.

Supervisor: Dr. Pankaj Kumar, Dept. of EES, IISER Bhopal

Research Intern — Spatial Downscaling Using CNNs

Climate & Glacier Modelling Lab (CGMLI), Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Bhopal, India

Apr 2021 – Jul 2021

Applied SRCNN and SRResNet-based image super-resolution techniques to spatially downscale IMD daily gridded rainfall data (1980–2010), benchmarking across multiple architectures. Published as a first-author comparative assessment in SN Computer Science.

Supervisor: Dr. Pankaj Kumar, Dept. of EES, IISER Bhopal
